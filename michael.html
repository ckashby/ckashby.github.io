This pdf resume is focused on Front-End Development.

I read your Web site and some of your blogs. I thought you would like to really get to know me. I read this in about 4 minutes.

This is my true story. I will do my best to be brief.

I am an experienced software developer, project manager, Oracle DBA and UI / UX designer looking for a company in Sonoma County where I can continue to develop my technical and people skills.

As a teenager, I fixed Apple II, Apple III, Compaq, IBM and Mac computers at ComputerLand. I became the Assistant Service Manager and a Senior Field Technician at 20.

I was fortunate to meet Marc Cantor and worked creating cutting-edge multimedia applications at Macromind / Macromedia using Director (now known as Adobe Flash). I created over a dozen multimedia CD-ROMs and a few touch-screen kiosks between 1988 and 1991.

I moved to Hawaii when I won the contract to produce the "Visit Hawaii CD-ROM" for the Hawaii Visitors and Conventions Bureau. This boot-strapped my software development company from 1991 - 1999. We managed multiple concurrent projects with up to a dozen team members and budgets of up to $250,000. Our clients included Aloha Airlines, Ala Moana Shopping Center, Budget Rent-a-Car, Kodak, Hilton Hotels, Hyatt Hotels and the Royal Hawaiian Shopping Center. When Tim Berners-Lee created the Web, we built most of these companies first Web sites.

In 2000 when the Web bubble crashed, I went to work as a Java programmer and project manager at the University of Hawaii. My team replaced 80+ proprietary applications with a Web-based HIPPA compliant electronic health record for the Department of Health. We also imported and indexed 10 years of Medicare data to create a data warehouse.

During that 7 year project, I became a certified Oracle Database Administrator (all five tests).

When Craig McClanahan created the Struts MVC framework, we transitioned our team from Java Beans to MVC using Struts and Ibatis. I also spearheaded the adoption of CSS, jQuery and Web components.

I contributed in a small way to Struts and Clinton Begin's Ibatis open-source project by editing the first tutorial and the manual. Ibatis is now a Google Code project.

I am also an experienced instructor, writer and editor. For the past few years, I have been mostly teaching surfing, kiteboarding and ukulele in Hawaii.

I have stayed on top of Web and Mobile development trends and technologies. Specifically, mobile first development, responsive design, single-page Web apps (Ember, Angular, React and Meteor) and I have published Android apps in the Google Play Store.

Last year, I edited the railstutorial.org for Michael Hartl and recently I edited the NG (Angular) book for Ari Lerner. I am currently editing a book on Android.

I have not used github lately because Android Studio has it's own version control system.

That's about it.

I recently returned to Petaluma to care for an aging family member.

Thank you for your time and interest.

Best wishes,
